Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth-largest moon in the solar system. It has been a subject of fascination and study for centuries, influencing various cultures, myths, and scientific discoveries. The Moon orbits Earth at an average distance of about 384,400 kilometers (238,855 miles), and its gravitational pull plays a crucial role in creating tides. It has a surface covered with craters, maria (large, dark, basaltic plains), and highlands, with no atmosphere to protect it from space debris or the Sun's radiation. The Moon's phases, from new moon to full moon, are caused by its position relative to Earth and the Sun.
